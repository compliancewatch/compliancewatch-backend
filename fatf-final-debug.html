  • High-risk and other monitored jurisdictions

    16 Jun 2025

    The FATF identifies jurisdictions with weak measures to combat money laundering and terrorist financing (AML/CFT) in two FATF public documents that are issued three times a year. Find out more about the process, and the countries that are currently on the so-called 'black and grey lists'.

  • Mutual Evaluations

    The mutual evaluations and follow-up reports as well as other documents related to the FATF's assessment process.

  • Methods and Trends

    10 May 2024

    FATFs research into existing or emerging money laundering and terrorist financing trends and methods will help countries better understand the risks they are exposed to.

  • Environmental Crime

    10 May 2024

    When properly implemented, the FATF Recommendations provide effective tools to go after the illicit financial flows from environmental crime.

  • Corruption

    10 May 2024

    Corruption offences, such as bribery or theft of public funds, are generally committed for the purpose of gaining illicit funds. The process of money laundering is to conceal that those funds were generated from criminal activity. That is why the FATF Standards are so important to the fight against corruption.

  • Financial inclusion and NPO issues

    10 May 2024

    Ensuring that financially excluded or underserved groups have access to regulated financial services helps to strengthen the implementation of AML/CFT measures.

  • FATF Recommendations

    19 Apr 2024
